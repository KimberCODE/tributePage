'Song for Littles'

Ways Mrs. Rachel helps parents with introducing speak to toddlers:

  • FOCUS on the articulation of speech sounds–Ms. Rachel’s mouth is front and center with the video camera likely inches away from her face.
  • FOCUS on teaching sign language for basic concepts such as ‘mama’, ‘dada’, ‘eat’, ‘all done’, and ‘more’.
  • FOCUS on repetition of vocabulary, concepts, sign language, movement, and fingerplays.
  • FOCUS on clapping phonemes and syllables which provides essential practice of phonological awareness .
  • FOCUS on social-emotional learning. Ms. Rachel is very consistent in terms of her upbeat delivery, consistency, positive cueing and body language.
  • FOCUS on providing parents and caregivers with good speech and oral language modeling.
  • FOCUS on language learning one bite at a time. Even though a typical video can run for 40-60 minutes, Ms. Rachel is careful to present songs and concepts in short segments without fanfare or visual overstimulation.

Wearing her signature bluejean overalls, pink T-shirt and a matching headband, she became that friendly woman from the videos: the one who joyfully pronounces words, babbles if necessary, waves and sings to instruct her little viewers.
